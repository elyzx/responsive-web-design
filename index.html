Anna Atkins

The first person to publish a book illustrated with photographic images

Here's a timeline of Anna Atkins life:

  • 1799 - Born in Tonbridge, Kent, England to John and Hester Anne Children.
  • 1825 - Married John Pelly Atkins, a London West India merchant.
  • 1839 - Elected a member of the London Botanical Society.
  • 1843 - Self-published her photograms in the first installment of Photographs of British Algae: Cyanotype Impressions. Although privately published, it is considered the first book illustrated with photographic images.
  • 1853 - Produced a total of three volumes of Photographs of British Algae: Cyanotype Impressions.
  • 1865 - Donated her collection of the algae, ferns and other plants that she used in her work to the British Museum.
  • 1871 - Died at Halstead Place in 1871 of "paralysis, rheumatism, and exhaustion" at the age of 72

In more recent years, Anna's work has been celebrated:

  • 1996 - One copy of the book with 411 plates in three volumes sold for £133,500 at auction.
  • 2004 - One copy of the book with 411 plates in three volumes sold for £133,500 at auction.
  • 2017 - Atkins work was a major feature in the New Realities Photography in the Nineteenth Century exhibition held in the Rijksmuseum, Amsterdam, June – September 2017
  • 2018 - The New York Public Library opened an exhibition on Atkins' life and work, featuring various versions of Photographs of British Algae.
